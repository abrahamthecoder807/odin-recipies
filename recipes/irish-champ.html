Irish champ

Description

Irish champ is similar to a colcannon but is made by blending green onions or scallions with creamy meshed potatoes. It's great on its own, served steaming hot with extra butter on top, but it's also a perfect dish for a good quality sausages.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds potatoes, peeled and halved
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 bunch green onions, thinly sliced.
  • ¼ cup butter, cut into pieces
  • ½ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps on how to make an Irish champ

  1. Place potatoes into a large pot and cover with salt water; bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, about 20 minutes.
  2. Drain potatoes well, then return tot he pot and cover with a clean dish towel. Place the pot over very low heat until potatoes are dry, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, heat the milk and green onion in a saucepan over medium-low heat until warm, 3 to 4 minutes
  4. Once potatoes are dry, add butter and salt; mash with a potatoes masher until smooth. Stir in warm milk mixture until well combined, then season with pepper.
